## ‚öñÔ∏è LEGAL REQUIREMENTS

### 1. Employee Consent (MANDATORY)
- **Inform employees** that they are being monitored
- **Get written consent** from all monitored employees
- **Explain clearly** what data is collected (screenshots, activity logs, etc.)
- **Provide opt-out** for personal devices

### 2. Privacy Laws Compliance
Different countries have different laws:

#### United States
- **Electronic Communications Privacy Act (ECPA)** - Requires consent
- **State laws vary** - Some states require two-party consent
- **Company-owned devices** - More flexibility, but still need policy

#### European Union (GDPR)
- **Article 88** - Employee monitoring must be proportionate
- **Data minimization** - Only collect necessary data
- **Transparency** - Employees must know what's collected
- **Right to access** - Employees can request their data
- **Fines** - Up to ‚Ç¨20 million or 4% of global revenue

#### United Kingdom
- **Data Protection Act 2018** - Similar to GDPR
- **ICO Guidelines** - Must have legitimate business reason
- **Impact Assessment** - Required for high-risk processing

#### Canada
- **PIPEDA** - Requires consent and transparency
- **Provincial laws** - Additional requirements in some provinces

#### Australia
- **Privacy Act 1988** - Requires notification
- **Workplace Surveillance Acts** - State-specific requirements

## üö´ PROHIBITED USES

**DO NOT use this system to:**
- Monitor employees without their knowledge (illegal in most jurisdictions)
- Capture personal devices without consent
- Monitor during breaks or personal time
- Discriminate or harass employees

## ‚úÖ BEST PRACTICES

### 1. Transparency First
```
"This computer is monitored for security and productivity purposes.
Screenshots are captured every 5 minutes.
Activity logs are recorded continuously.
By using this computer, you acknowledge this monitoring.
For questions, contact HR at [email]"
```

### 2. Minimize Data Collection
- Only capture what's necessary
- Blur sensitive areas (passwords, personal info)
- Shorter retention periods
- Delete data when employee leaves

### 3. Secure Storage
- Encrypt screenshots at rest
- Encrypt transmission to server
- Role-based access controls
- Audit logs for data access
- Regular security reviews

### 4. Employee Trust
- Be transparent about monitoring
- Allow employees to view their data
- Respond to concerns promptly
- Use data fairly (not for harassment)
- Regular policy reviews with employees

## üîß TECHNICAL SAFEGUARDS

### Implement These Features:

1. **Sensitive Data Detection**
   - OCR to detect passwords, credit cards
   - Automatic blurring of sensitive areas
   - Blacklist certain applications (banking, medical)

   - Require consent before monitoring starts
   - Log consent acceptance with timestamp
   - Allow consent withdrawal

3. **Data Minimization**
   - Configurable screenshot intervals
   - Option to disable screenshots (activity logs only)
   - Automatic deletion after retention period

4. **Access Controls**
   - Multi-factor authentication for admin access
   - Audit logs for all data access
   - Role-based permissions

5. **Employee Dashboard**
   - Let employees view their own data
   - Download their data (GDPR right)
   - Request data deletion
